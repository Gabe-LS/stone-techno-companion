Confirmed no keyboard support exists for room-list items. I now have enough to compile the accessibility findings for the meetup feature.

## Findings

- ID: a11y-1
- Severity: high
- Confidence: certain
- Location: `server/chat/chat.html:3224-3263` (`openMeetupModal`)
- Finding: The "Create Meetup" modal is built as a plain `<div class="modal-overlay">`/`<div class="modal">` with no `role="dialog"`, no `aria-modal="true"`, no `aria-labelledby` pointing at the "Create Meetup" title, no focus trap (Tab freely moves focus to elements behind the overlay), and no focus-return to the trigger element (the "+" action-menu's "Create Meetup" button) when the modal closes. This directly contradicts the project's own documented HTML standard ("Modals: role=dialog, aria-modal, aria-labelledby; focus returns to trigger on close; tab trapping; Escape closes"). Escape does close it (global `keydown` handler at chat.html:4675-4680), but that's the only part of the pattern that's implemented. Screen-reader users get no indication a dialog opened, and keyboard users can tab out into the underlying chat UI while the modal is "open".
- Recommendation: Add `role="dialog" aria-modal="true" aria-labelledby="meetup-modal-title"` to the modal element, add `id="meetup-modal-title"` to the `.modal-title` span, capture `document.activeElement` before opening and restore focus to it in `closeMeetupModal()`, and add a Tab/Shift+Tab focus trap scoped to the modal's focusable children (title input → date → hour → minute → location → GPS button → note → close/create buttons).
- Effort: M
- Risk of change: low

- ID: a11y-2
- Severity: high
- Confidence: certain
- Location: `server/chat/chat.html:3233-3248`
- Finding: Every field in the meetup modal (Title, Date & Time, Location, Note) uses a bare `<label class="modal-label">Text</label>` immediately followed by a sibling `<input>`/`<select>`/`<textarea>` — the label has no `for` attribute and doesn't wrap the control, so there is no programmatic label/control association. This is worst for the Hour and Minute `<select id="meetup-hour">`/`<select id="meetup-min">`: both are populated with the same `00`–`23` / `00,15,30,45` style options and share one visually-adjacent "Date & Time" label, so a screen-reader user tabbing through them hears two unlabeled listboxes with numeric values and cannot tell which one sets the hour vs. the minute.
- Recommendation: Add `for`/`id` pairs (e.g. `<label for="meetup-title">`) for Title/Location/Note, and give the hour/minute selects explicit `aria-label="Hour"` / `aria-label="Minute"` (or wrap each in its own `<label>`).
- Effort: S
- Risk of change: low

- ID: a11y-3
- Severity: high
- Confidence: certain
- Location: `server/chat/chat.html:3284-3296` (`submitMeetup`), `server/static/shared.js:43-59` (`showToast`)
- Finding: All feedback for the meetup-creation flow — "Please enter a title", "Please pick a date", "Could not get location" (GPS failure), and the success confirmation "Meetup created!" — is delivered exclusively via `showToast`, whose element carries no `role="status"`/`role="alert"`/`aria-live` region. A screen-reader user who submits an incomplete form, or whose GPS lookup fails, receives no announcement at all that anything happened; the modal simply stays open (or silently closes on success) with no accessible signal. Focus also isn't moved back to the offending field on validation failure.
- Recommendation: Give the toast container `role="status" aria-live="polite"` (or `role="alert"` for errors) so assistive tech announces its text automatically; on validation failure in `submitMeetup`, also move focus to the invalid field (e.g. `document.getElementById('meetup-title').focus()`).
- Effort: S
- Risk of change: low

- ID: a11y-4
- Severity: high
- Confidence: certain
- Location: `server/chat/chat.html:1858` (main list), `server/chat/chat.html:2745` (menu drawer list), `server/chat/chat.html:1688` (general room list, same pattern)
- Finding: Meetup entries are rendered as `<li class="room-item" onclick="openRoom(...)">` — a click-only handler on a non-interactive element with no `tabindex="0"`, `role="button"`, or keyboard handler. This contradicts the project's documented HTML standard ("Interactive elements have tabindex=\"0\" role=\"button\" + keyboard handlers"). A keyboard-only or screen-reader user cannot focus or activate a meetup list item to open its room at all — the only reachable controls inside it are the bell button (and, in the menu variant, nothing).
- Recommendation: Add `tabindex="0" role="button"` to the `<li>` and a keydown handler that triggers `openRoom(...)` on `Enter`/`Space`, matching the pattern used elsewhere for other interactive rows.
- Effort: M
- Risk of change: low

- ID: a11y-5
- Severity: medium
- Confidence: certain
- Location: `server/chat/chat.html:1644-1648`
- Finding: The "Rooms / Meetups / DMs" tab bar (the primary way to reach the meetup list) is three plain `<button>` elements toggled via a `.active` class, with no `role="tablist"` on the container, no `role="tab"`/`aria-selected` on the buttons, and no `role="tabpanel"` on the content region that swaps underneath. Screen-reader users get no indication that "Meetups" is a tab, whether it's currently selected, or that the panel content changed after activating it.
- Recommendation: Add `role="tablist"` to `.tabs`, `role="tab"` + `aria-selected="true|false"` + `aria-controls` to each button (updated in `switchTab`), and `role="tabpanel"` to the content container.
- Effort: M
- Risk of change: low

- ID: a11y-6
- Severity: medium
- Confidence: certain
- Location: `server/chat/chat.html:111,2220` (`.card-meta` on `.msg-card.card-meetup`), color tokens at `chat.html:23` and `server/static/shared.css:16`
- Finding: The meetup invite card's time line (`<div class="card-meta">${fmtTime(...)}</div>`) renders `--color-muted` (#6b7280) text on the meetup card's own background `--user-1-bg` (#FEE2E2). Computed contrast ratio is ≈3.95:1, below the WCAG 2.1 AA minimum of 4.5:1 for normal-size (12px) text — this fails on the one card variant where the project's blanket claim ("all text/icon colors pass WCAG 2.1 AA") doesn't hold, because `--color-muted` was evidently tuned against the default neutral card background, not the pink meetup-specific one.
- Recommendation: Use a darker muted color specifically for text on `.card-meetup` (e.g. a darkened variant of `--user-1` background family, or reuse `--color-text` at reduced weight) so contrast clears 4.5:1, or bump `--color-muted` for this card override only.
- Effort: S
- Risk of change: low

- ID: a11y-7
- Severity: medium
- Confidence: certain
- Location: `server/chat/chat.html:75` (`.room-bell` CSS), used by the meetup bell/mute toggle at `chat.html:1864`
- Finding: `.room-bell { padding: 0; ... }` gives the button no padding, so its hit area is exactly the 16×16px inline SVG — well under the ~44×44 CSS px minimum recommended for touch targets (and below WCAG 2.5.5/2.5.8). This is the only affordance to enable/mute notifications for a specific meetup from the list, and it sits directly next to the badge/room-item click target, making mis-taps likely on mobile, which is the primary device for this festival app.
- Recommendation: Add `min-width: 44px; min-height: 44px;` (or equivalent padding) to `.room-bell` while keeping the visual icon size unchanged, centering the icon within the larger hit area.
- Effort: S
- Risk of change: low

- ID: a11y-8
- Severity: low
- Confidence: certain
- Location: `server/chat/chat.html:1826-1832` (`.tab-dot`, `.menu-dot`)
- Finding: Unread-activity indicators on the "Meetups" tab and the menu-drawer "Meetups" section header are purely visual colored dots (`.tab-dot.active` / `.menu-dot.active`) toggled with no accompanying text, `aria-label`, or `aria-live` announcement. Screen-reader users have no way to learn that there's unread meetup activity without manually opening the tab.
- Recommendation: Add a visually-hidden text node (e.g. `<span class="sr-only"> · unread</span>`) toggled alongside the dot, or expose the count via `aria-label` on the tab button.
- Effort: S
- Risk of change: low

- ID: a11y-9
- Severity: low
- Confidence: certain
- Location: `server/chat/chat.html:3230, 2921, 3308, 3493, 3999, 4156` (all `.modal-close` buttons, meetup modal included)
- Finding: Every modal close button, including the meetup modal's, renders only the glyph `&times;` (×) as its content with no `aria-label`. Depending on the screen reader/font, this is announced as "×", "multiplication sign", or nothing meaningful — not "Close".
- Recommendation: Add `aria-label="Close"` to `.modal-close` buttons.
- Effort: S
- Risk of change: low

- ID: a11y-10
- Severity: low
- Confidence: speculative
- Location: `server/chat/chat.html:2223` (`.meetup-join.meetup-join-wide`), `server/chat/chat.html:3192-3195` (`loadMeetupJoinState`)
- Finding: The Join/Leave toggle button communicates its pressed state only through its visible text ("Join" ↔ "Joined") and a CSS class; it has no `aria-pressed`. Most screen readers re-announce a focused button's new accessible name after an in-place text update, so this likely works in practice, but it isn't a semantically correct toggle button and behavior isn't guaranteed across all AT/browser combinations, especially since the state can also change out-of-band (another device joining doesn't update this button, but that's a sync issue, not a labeling one).
- Recommendation: Add `aria-pressed="true|false"` alongside the text update in `loadMeetupJoinState`/`toggleMeetupJoin`.
- Effort: S
- Risk of change: low
